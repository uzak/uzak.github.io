Nietzsche’s distinction between Kraft (“force” or “strength”) and Macht (“power” or “might”). Kraft is primordial strength that may be exercised by anything possessing it, while Macht is, within Nietzsche’s philosophy, closely tied to sublimation and “self-overcoming”, the conscious channeling of Kraft for creative purposes. (Will to power)
