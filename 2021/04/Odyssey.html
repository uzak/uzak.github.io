Assorted Thoughts

  • Odysseus (“at odds with another”), king of Ithaca, is called to service in the Trojan War at the same time as his son Telemachus is born.
    • Odysseus is Ulysses - man with the tight wound (initiation -> identity).
    • Troy - tripura
      • Priam - priya
      • Paris - slayer of Achilles (sorrow -> kama); pEra -> bag, pouch; peras -> Threshold, Limes; barge (gr:baris). “Defender”.
      • Helena - the shining one; mistress of sunlight; torch. Light enclosed in Troy - sharira trayam.
      • Hector - to have, to hold; to check, to restrain. Represents security - artha.
    • Has to leave his bow at home for his son, to whom it now belongs, so he can teach him how to use it once he returns. Several times Telemachus tries to string it in Odysseus’ absence, each time unsuccessfully.
    • Before leaving he puts the head of a sheep on Athena’s altar.
    • Athena wants him to go to war. Mother asks him to turn Troy to ashes. He says to Penelope: “Troy will not fall easily”.
    • Telemachus - nous; logos; intellect; intuition in tuition. He will inherit the kingdom once his beard grows (meaning he’s mature). Meaning: “far from battle”.
  • Ship - body identification. Inhabited by his crew - all his mental deficiencies, vices.
  • Achilles: achos - distress, pain, sorrow; laos - people, soldiers, nation
  • Odysseus’ ego gets the best of him and he tells the gods that he did it himself:
    • O: See, the gods of Heaven and the Sea! I conquered Troy! Me, Odysseus! I am irresistible!
    • P: Odysseus, why are you challenging me?
    • O: Who are you?
    • P: It’s me, Poseidon
    • O: For ten years, you played with us as with toys. For ten years, you left your shores drink the blood!
    • P: But they are my servants who have reduced to silence Laocoon when your horse was doomed. Now you refuse to thank me. You forget that men are nothing without the gods. For this offense, you will know suffering. Because of your arrogance, you will be tossed on the sea for eternity. You will never see again Ithaca.
    • O: You cannot stop!
    • P: You will know pain Odysseus!
  • Trojan horse: wood (materia), giant (ego).
  • Cyclops Polypemus (“abounding in songs and legends”), traps them in his cave. Odysseus gets him drunk and blinds him with a huge stake. Hiding under sheep skins he and his men escape. Odysseus brashly taunts the cyclops who asks his father Poseidon to avenge him.
  • Odysseus is not yet happy to be nobody, so he brags to be Odysseus.
  • Aeolus gives bag of wind to help to get home. One of his men opens it prematurely.
  • Peremedes (“very cunning”) is one of the most loyal companions to Odysseus.
  • Circle: sorceress, who turns men into animals and blackmails him into sleeping with her. Hermes helps to escape. 5 years instead of 5 days. Odysseus digs his ship out of the sand and sails to the Underworld.
  • Underworld. Blind prophet Tiresias recognizes his wit and courage, criticize his ego and foolishness.
  • Anticlea (“without fame”) tells Odysseus there there are multiple suitors for Penelope, wanting her for her money and power.
  • Road home goes beyond Scylla (sea monster) and Charybdis (tidal pool).
  • Scylla’s six head kill many. Charybdis creates whirlpool and destroys the ship. Odysseus arrives alone on the navel-like island of Calypso. There are only women there.
  • Athena tells the now 15 year old Telemachus to go to Sparta and find out about his Father.
  • Two years later Hermes arranges with Calypso (“she who conceals”) to have Odysseus released and she provides him with driftwood for a raft to Ithaca. Journey goes now: Ogygia (Omphalos of the Talassaean sea) -> Scheria (also known as “Phaeacia”).
  • Poseidon reminds Odysseus about what he say on the day he left Troy and to remember his place as mortal:
    • P: Always in trouble, all over the seas, wherever you go, Odysseus!
    • O: Poseidon What do you want?! What do you want from me?
    • P: I want you to suffer
    • O: I have nothing. All what is left is my life.
    • P: My goal is not to kill you. You must understand.
    • O: What do you want me to understand
    • P: That without gods man is nothing!
  • Alcinous (“mighty mind”) happy ruler of Phaeacians, who has by Arete (“she who is prayed for, sacred, righteous”) five sons and one daughter Nausicaa (“burner of ships”) helps Odysseus back to Ithaca. Having listened to his story, the Phaeacians agree to provide Odysseus with more treasure than he would have received from the spoils of Troy. They deliver him at night while he is fast asleep. On the next morning he himself gets united with Telemachus.
  • I am a man of Mnaemae and Phrenae, Mnaemo is my name, and Peirasis is my Game.
  • Once the suitors (108) are dead, Odysseus is finally reunited with Penelope.
    • Penelope: Odysseus.
    • Odysseus: I will never leave you.
    • Penelope: I have changed a lot …
    • Odysseus: No… You’re still the same.
    • Penelope: You’re still a liar, Odysseus! You have not seen me for twenty years …
    • Odysseus: No, one day…
    • Odysseus: Penelope: In one day you have seen the whole world?
    • Odysseus: Yes. I saw the sacred and the beautiful …but nothing can match the treasure of a man …A treasure he holds in his hands …and will be his forever. You are my world
  • Nausicaa later married Odysseus’s son Telemachus, and had a son, Poliporthes (“destroyer of cities” - tripurantaka).
  • The scar on his tight that Odysseus sustained in the childhood hunting a wild boar identifies him. Once he shoots the arrow through the 12 double-axes he’s identity is clear. Through the scar immaculate birth was given.
