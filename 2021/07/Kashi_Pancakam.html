Class 1

  • Small prakarana by Shankaracarya
  • pravaha - flow, activity, current.
  • When it comes to knowledge, there no division between east and west.
  • Nature of work is grow, until it occupies all free time you have.
  • It is the means that shapes the end. You won’t get the end independent of the means
  • If bhakti is separates from philosophy, it becomes superstitious. Religion loses its transforming power. Society comes into focus, not individual liberation.
  • We rise above literality of the mythology and rise to the spirit. That’s why it is called spirituality. If you take them literally, you once again fall into the trap of establishing division.
  • The rishis love to cloud the truth in a symbolic language. Instead of telling that sun’s white light can be split into seven colours, the mantras say that “he travels by a chariot pulled by seven horses”. sa:sapti - horse, ray of light.
  • Serpent going and eating part of the sun - shadow. It covers up part of the sun or the moon.
  • In all this stories the literality has to be appreciated and spirit must be understood correctly. Then only there is beauty in those stories.
  • Kashi - atmajnanam in this context.
  • sa:nija - self
  • I’m is the most fundamental experience of every human being. It is prior to everything else, incl. god.
  • I’m is the primordial intelligence. “God is” is not, neither “you’re”.
  • What is the matrix of I’m? Conscious being. That conscious being is the Kashi - because it is self-shining. You validate the existence of all objects. “I’m-ness” is svayamprakasha - it is prior to perception and thinking.
  • Body makes you limited in space, mind in time.
  • If god is a limited guy, he can have only one center. If he’s spherical. If he’s elipsoid, he can have two. Whereas every human heart is the center of God. He’s infinite.
  • Most important, sacred spot in Kashi is the place where bodies are burned.
  • You will not be able to invite the truth. It will reveal itself. You only have to prepare yourself for the revelation. The preparation is negation of the identification with the body, which is threefold: me, mine, for me. aham mama mahyam. Mahyam gets neutralised by karma yoga. Mama, things related to me, are neutralised by bhakti yoga. Everything is of Ishvara, nothing is mine. In bhakti namah (na mama) is the most important thing. Aham karta is negated by sankhya yoga -> nirahamkara.
  • Without crucification there can be no resurrection. What resurrects is the Christ who is one with the God - myself and father in the heaven are one and the same. How to do? Begin with namahyam. Then namama.
  • Upashanti: shanti shanti shanti is 3x because there are three kinds of papas. Adhiatmika, adhibauthika, adhidaivika. Each papam is a fire, heat, pain, suffering. Shanti douses all three of them. The heart is at peace.
  • But Real shanti is not upashanti. It is parashanti, moksha. Permanent freedom from suffering - nirvana. It is a negative word. It is not a positive definition. I cannot say what it is itself.
  • Silence per se is not important. When the mind is moving, the flow of thoughts is harmless if you know that the content of the mind is unreal.
  • You psychologically depend on that content of waking consciousness for self-fulfillment and happiness and take it to be other. It is a very far and deep ignorance. It can give me sukha and dukha and therefore to have more sukha and less dukha I have to manipulate the content. The flow of mind throws a screen of thought over the truth which is then totally covered.
  • You have to discover the truth deep within so you can compare it to the mind created world. The mind creates a world for us and presents it to us every day morning. We’re accepting that world and take it to be real and ourselves to be a small part of the world.
  • Rather than taking up the struggle to discover the truth ourselves it is very convenient to handle the responsibility for the truth to the mahatma and start relaxing. How do you know that jagat is mithya? Sastra says that … c’mon! How long will you manage like this? Therefore gain the paramopashanti. That is the real kashi. You get by manonivrtti.
  • Suppose I see a pot. Now I pay attention to the pot. And now I’m interested in the pot. That makes the pot real. While paying attention and investing interest cannot say a thing is mithya. Stop paying attention and interest and see what happens. That is called manonivrtti. Just watch your mind in an uninvolved way. Be kind to it. Do it constantly.
  • De-automatize everything. Like stop talking when you are eating food. Because if you are talking, eating becomes automatic and the attention is on talking and there is identification. It is the mind that is functioning through the taste-buds. Just watch it.
  • Just watch the mind. Don’t ask how. Suppose you find an agitation in the mind. When the mind is agitated I’m agitated. That is called identification. There is no viveka (between I’m and the mind). I’m and I think are not the same. When you watch the mind it becomes pure. Slowly. Once the mind is pure you’ll know very spontaneously that you’re not the mind but the witness of the mind.
  • Transcending the mind means knowing that I’m not the mind, antahkarana. Antahkarana suddhi is the most important starting point.
  • That silence is conscious presence. It is not a gap, it is not void. It is the kashi. In Kashi there is a flow of ganga. Ganga is pure and also purifying. In the Kashi which is in my heart, the Ganga is jnanapravaha.
  • I’m the primordial light/intelligence. The moment body comes into the light the world also comes. They always come together and resolve together.
  • The light is the atmajnanu. The brilliantly shining awareness. The Kashi. And the playfulness (vilasa) of the mind is the film. Steven Spielberg knows it, that’s why he called his company Dreamworks.
  • The world you experience is entirely, not partly, a projection.
  • Whatever is the content of consciousness, whatever is known to you as part of that content, that is not I’m. I’m is unknown, unknowable, undefinable, undesignated, undifferentiated, primordial awareness of being.
  • You don’t want money, wife and children … what you’re seeking in life is happiness. You’ve a right to seek it. Seeking is right, but the method is wrong. You don’t seek happiness in the other. The other is maya and kalpita (imaginary). You see a movie and feel good. Where do you get that feeling of goodness? From the screen it flows to you? No, from you it flows onto the screen.
  • Discover it: be sat cit. Then discover the fullness of that. Then as you discover it, the screen of thought projected by the mind will slowly move away. And the inner silence starts flowing into the life and makes it sacred, joyful.
  • When you say I’m rich you’re deriving the sense of self from the riches. When you say I’m the husband you’re deriving the sense of self from your status. That’s wrong. Be the love of the husband. Don’t derive the sense of self from anything that you know. Don’t have any identification what so ever.
  • Mahatmas find it so hard to identify with anything. Samsaris people readily identify with anything what-so-ever. Be a true nobody, not somebody, then the real self shines in all its glory. It is called paramatmarupa.
